## Podcast Sound Design — Permanently Wired

`assemble_podcast.sh` is now called as step 2b in `otto-daily-some` cron. Every morning: ElevenLabs TTS → assemble_podcast.sh mixes intro sting + music bed + voiceover + outro → `-final.mp3` goes to Slack/Libby/website. If assembly fails, briefing still goes out without sound design (graceful fallback).

**Quality standard:** 192kbps CBR stereo 44.1kHz. Industry standard for high-quality podcast. Not VBR — CBR ensures consistent bitrate for streaming. ElevenLabs source is 128kbps mono; assembled output is an upgrade.

**Timing design (locked in):** 9s intro segment. Music fades over 4 seconds (5s–9s). 3-second acrossfade overlap — Otto starts speaking at second 6, music still ~50%, fades to silence by second 3 of his speech. 1.5s outro crossfade into typewriter click. This is the right feel — not a hard cut dressed as a transition.

## Weekly Some EP3 Silent Gap — Root Cause & Fix

80-second silent gap at 1:30–1:35 was corrupt MP3 frame header in Part 1 of ElevenLabs two-part generation. ffmpeg concat joined the files but Part 1 had corrupt header causing complete audio dropout until Part 2 resumed.

**Fix:** Re-encode each ElevenLabs part to uniform format before concat. This is now documented in assemble_podcast.sh header as a permanent note. Always re-encode parts individually before joining — prevents this class of bug entirely regardless of API response quality.
